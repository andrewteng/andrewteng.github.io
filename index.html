I received my bachelor’s degree from Emory University in mathematics and computer science, while on a pre-med track as I was interested in bridging the gap between health sciences and computer science. By combining these two trades, I attended the bioinformatics master’s program at Georgia Tech. During my time there, I was a graduate research assistant in the Lachance Lab and focused on utilizing genome-wide association studies in our analysis of elevated prostate cancer risk in men of African descent and aspects of ascertainment bias. While at Georgia Tech, I interned at the National Cancer Institute under Dr. Kai Yu and focused on analyzing and creating a prediction model to detect cervical cancer risk in patients with HPV from pap smear results. Currently, I am pursuing my PhD at the University of Washington School of Medicine under the biomedical and health informatics program. I am interested in analyzing electronic health/medical records to improve quality of care and outcomes in health related settings.

Genome characterization of human papillomavirus (HPV) 16 
Summer Project, Mentor: Dr. Kai Yu
Full genome analysis and exploration of HPV 16 to create a prediction model to forecast cervical cancer likelihood and risk. The primary focus was to identify the viral genetic basis of HPV carcinogenicity and to globally analyze the differences patients with and without cervical cancer through random forest classification.
