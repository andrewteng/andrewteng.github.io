As a graduate of the bioinformatics master's program at Georgia Tech, I have deep research interests in precision medicine and personal genomics. With a bachelor's degree in mathematics and computer science science from Emory Uiversity, my primary interest is to bridge the gap between health sciences and computer science.

During my time at Georgia Tech, I was a graduate research assistant in the Lachance Lab and focused on utilizing genome-wide association studies in our analysis of prostate cancer in men of African descent. Last summer, I interned at the National Cancer Institute under Dr. Kai Yu and focused on creating a prediction model to detect cervical cancer in patients with HPV. I will be pursing my PhD at the University of Washington School of Medicine under the biomedical and health informatics PhD program.
