I am a recent graduate of the bioinformatics master's program at Georgia Tech and graduated from Emory University with a bachelor's degree in mathematics and computer science while following a pre-med track. My primary interest is to bridge the gap between health sciences and computer science; I have deep research interests in precision medicine and personal genomics. During my time at Georgia Tech, I was a graduate research assistant in the Lachance Lab and focused on utilizing genome-wide association studies in our analysis of prostate cancer in men of African descent. This past summer, I interned at the National Cancer Institute under Dr. Kai Yu and focused on creating a prediction model to detect cervical cancer in human papillomavirus patients. I will be pursing my PhD at the University of Washington School of Medicine under the biomedical and health informatics PhD program.

Cheers!
